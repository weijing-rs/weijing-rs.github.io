Highlighted Researches

  • Estimation of near-surface fine particles

    Space-Time Extremely randomized Trees (STET) model

    We have developed a new space–time extremely randomized trees (STET) model by integrating spatiotemporal information and additional auxiliary data (including AOD, meteorological, topographical, land-use data and pollution emissions) to improve the spatial resolution and overall accuracy of PM estimates across China. Based on this model, we have generated long-term, high-resolution and high-quality near-surface fine particle (e.g., PM1, PM2.5, PM10), in China, i.e., ChinaHighPMx dataset. This dataset will be useful in future atmospheric pollution studies focused on medium- or small-scale areas.

  • Aerosol optical depth retrieval

    (1) RRHA algorithm

    We have developed a regionally robust high-resolution aerosol retrieval (RRHA) algorithm for different satellites (e.g., Landsat, MODIS, VIIRS, et al.) over Eastern China which has complex surfaces and severe air pollution by solving several major challenges in aerosol retrieval including: i) surface reflectance by correcting for the effects of surface bidirectional reflectance distribution function using the RossThick-LiSparse model; ii) aerosol models assumed by time-series data analysis with historical aerosol optical properties measurements; iii) cloud screening using our proposed more accurate cloud mask algorithms; and iv) gas (i.e., ozone and water vapor) absorption correctiong using improved modeled approximations. The uncertainties of regional AOD estimates have significantly reducted and the overall accuracy has been significantly improved.

    Reference:

    [1] Wei, J., Li, Z., Peng, Y., Sun, L., and Yan, X. A regionally robust high-spatial-resolution aerosol retrieval algorithm for MODIS images over Eastern China. IEEE Transactions on Geoscience and Remote Sensing, 2019, 57(7), 4748–4757. https://doi.org/10.1109/TGRS.2019.2892813

    [2] Wei, J., Li, Z., Sun, L., Yang, Y., Zhao, C., and Cai, Z. Enhanced aerosol estimations from Suomi-NPP VIIRS images over heterogeneous surfaces. IEEE Transactions on Geoscience and Remote Sensing, 2019, 57(12), 9534–9543. https://doi.org/10.1109/TGRS.2019.2927432

    [3] Wei, J., Huang, B., Sun, L., Zhang, Z., Wang, L., and Bilal, M. A simple and universal aerosol retrieval algorithm for Landsat series images over complex surfaces. Journal of Geophysical Research Atmospheres, 2017, 122(24), 13338–13355. https://doi.org/10.1002/2017JD026922

    • (2) I-HARLS algorithm

    We have developed an improved aerosol retrieval algorithm for MODIS images at 1-km spatial resolution, which is is based on the high-resolution aerosol retrieval algorithm with a priori land surface reflectance database support (HARLS), which was developed over bright urban areas and was subsequently modified and validated over land. For this study, an eight-day surface reflectance database and a seasonal aerosol-type database over land are constructed using the MODIS surface reflectance and aerosol products. Then, the 1 km resolution global AOD dataset is first generated. This dataset can be used for air-pollution- and climate-related studies at medium or small scales.

    Reference:

  • Cloud detection for remote sensing images

    (1) RFmask algorithm

    we have developed a new algorithm called the Random-Forest-based cloud mask (RFmask), which can improve the accuracy of cloud identifcation from different Landsat images (e.g., TM, ETM+, and OLI). We frst chose the stratifed sampling method to pre-select cloudy and clear-sky pixels to form a prior-pixel database according to the land use cover around the world. Next, we select typical spectral channels and calculate spectral indices as inputs to the RF model for training and establishing a preliminary cloud detection model. Finally, the Super-pixels Extracted via Energy-Driven Sampling (SEEDS) segmentation approach is applied to re-process the preliminary classifcation results to obtain the fnal cloud detection results. The RFmask algorithm is not only accurate but also computationally efcient. It is potentially useful for a variety of applications in using Landsat data, especially for monitoring land cover and land-use changes.

    Reference: Wei, J., Huang, W., Li, Z., Sun, L., Zhu, X., Yuan, Q., Liu, L., and Cribb, M. Cloud detection for Landsat imagery by combining the random forest and super-pixels extracted via energy-driven sampling segmentation approaches. Remote Sensing of Environment, 2020, 248, 112005. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rse.2020.112005

    • (2) UDTCDA algorithm

    We have developed a new Universal Dynamic Threshold Cloud Detection Algorithm (UDTCDA) supported by a priori surface reflectance database for different satellites (e.g., MODIS, VIIRS, Landsat, et al.). A monthly surface reflectance database is constructed using long-time-sequenced MODerate resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer surface reflectance product (MOD09A1) to provide the surface reflectance of the underlying surfaces. The relationships between the apparent reflectance changes and the surface reflectance are simulated under different observation and atmospheric conditions with the 6S (Second Simulation of the Satellite Signal in the Solar Spectrum) model, and the dynamic threshold cloud detection models are developed. The UDTCDA can effectively reduce the effects of atmospheric factors and mixed pixels and can be applied to different satellite sensors to realize long-term, large-scale cloud detection operations

    Reference: Sun, L., Wei, J.*, Wang, J., Mi, X., Guo, Y., Lv, Y., Yang, Y., Gan, P., Zhou, X., Jia, C., and Tian, X. A universal dynamic threshold cloud detection algorithm (UDTCDA) supported by a prior surface reflectance database. Journal of Geophysical Research Atmospheres, 2016, 121(12), 7172–7196. https://doi.org/10.1002/2015JD024722.
